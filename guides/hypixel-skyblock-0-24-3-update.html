Hypixel SkyBlock 0.24.3 Update Guide

Hypixel SkyBlock 0.24.3 is not a one-feature patch. It touches daily routing, fishing, greenhouse farming, Glacite Mineshaft hunting and the Abiphone system at the same time. That is why a good summary has to do two jobs: separate the official changes from creator interpretation and then translate those changes into practical decisions about progression and market timing.

Primary source base: the official Hypixel 0.24.3 thread first, then creator recap coverage for the early day-one player view. When the two sources differ in confidence, this page follows the official thread.

The biggest confirmed changes

The official thread introduced 25 new Abiphone contacts and expanded Abiphone trio capacity, which matters more than it first sounds because it changes how many everyday NPC conveniences players can keep active at once. The same patch added Littlefoot's Den in the Glacite Mineshafts, along with new Littlefoot rewards including the Littlefoot Shard and Littlefoot's Fluff for the Baby Yeti mythic upgrade path. Fishing also got broad support: Jerry Island was refreshed, hotspots were added, winter fishing behavior became clearer and several item and stat systems were cleaned up.

Farming quietly got some of the most durable QoL. The greenhouse systems became easier to read through mutation previews, fully grown crop effects stayed locked until ready and the Cultivation requirement was reduced. The Reforge Anvil was also discontinued, which is the kind of systems cleanup that does not dominate thumbnails but absolutely changes player expectations.

What markets deserve attention first

The creator recap immediately flagged some obvious early pressure points: Abiphone contact materials, Bayou Water Orb, Epic Tarantula Pet pricing and the new Littlefoot-related drops. Patch-week logic says to watch those, but not trust them blindly. Fishing materials, Jerry Island items and greenhouse inputs can all move too, especially because several of those systems now feed broader progression loops rather than one isolated recipe.

Use SkyCofl top movers to spot the first big moves, SkyCofl low supply to see whether a spike is just shallow listings, BazaarPro market trends to compare momentum, BazaarTracker demand to check if buyers are really clearing the item and Skyblock.bz manipulation when the chart starts looking theatrical. SkyCofl and BazaarPro are both useful here for different reasons: SkyCofl is excellent for fast visibility, and BazaarPro is good for reading the larger trend context without romanticizing one spike.

Best day-one checklist

If you are logging in after the patch, the clean route is straightforward. Update your Abiphone contacts, test the fishing outpost changes, check whether you can unlock or expand greenhouse progress, and only then decide whether any market move is worth chasing. If you run Glacite Mineshafts, add Littlefoot to your watchlist, but treat those drops like a measured hunt rather than free money.

The broad lesson of 0.24.3 is that many small friction cuts were removed at once. That usually creates a more stable long-term economy than a single flashy item patch. Players who do best are the ones who adapt their workflow first and speculate second.
