## Combat changes that matter for coins

The Crown of Avarice now gains a 2× coin multiplier once its counter reaches 1 billion. This is not a small perk — for players who already farm coins through combat, it effectively doubles output at the top end. The crown's market price reacted accordingly, and anyone holding one before the patch announcement got a clean windfall.

The Life Recovery attribute cooldown was increased from 0.2s to 0.5s, which slightly reduces sustain in prolonged combat loops. For most players this is barely noticeable, but for the highest-intensity slayer and dungeon builds, it means one less safety net. The Tormentor armor adjustment — swapping two Sapphire gemstone slots for Combat slots and adding +30 Strength — shifts it toward more aggressive builds and away from pure intelligence scaling.

## Slayer drop table cleanup

Several items moved from slayer extra tables to the main drop pool: Festering Maggot, Enchanted Blaze Powder (with weight reduced to 1500), and High Class Archfiend Dice. This matters because main-table items drop more reliably and are less RNG-gated. For slayer grinders, this means more consistent profit per boss and less variance-driven frustration. The Enchanted Blaze Powder weight reduction is especially welcome — it was previously too rare for an ingredient that feeds multiple crafting chains.

## Dungeon drop messages and Croesus visibility

Rare dungeon items now trigger floor-wide drop messages, and particularly desirable drops like the Necron's Handle get an even more visible variant that also appears at Croesus when opening chests. This is a pure QoL win — it makes dungeons feel more social, gives parties a shared moment when a big drop happens, and helps players verify that rare items are actually dropping in their runs. From a market perspective, better visibility into drop frequency could slightly reduce misinformation-driven price spikes.

## Pet changes with economic ripple effects

The Horse Pet and Skeleton Horse Pet were converted from ridable mobs into skull items with new perks. This sounds cosmetic but changes their utility profile — they are no longer mountable but gain new stat bonuses. Players who used them for mobility will need alternatives; players who held them for collection value should check the new perk list to see if demand shifts.

Jerry Boxes now go directly into inventory instead of dropping on the floor, eliminating the risk of losing boxes to despawn timers or lobby swaps. Soulflow Engines now work with Catalysts, opening up new minion optimization routes. The quiver arrow now displays current arrow stats in its item lore, making it much easier to compare arrow upgrades without external tools.
