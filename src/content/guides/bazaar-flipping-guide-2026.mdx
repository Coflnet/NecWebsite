## Advanced mode is not optional

If you want better bazaar decisions, turn on the information the default interface hides. Recent insta-buy and insta-sell data matters because it tells you whether a spread belongs to an active market or to an item with almost no real movement. A wide spread on a dead product is not a beginner flip. It is a waiting room with better marketing.

This is also why the strongest creator advice keeps sounding boring. High-volume items with stable order flow are rarely flashy, but they let you recycle capital. In practice, that makes them better than dramatic percentage spreads on niche products that only trade in tiny bursts.

## The spread is the job

Every good bazaar flip is a timing trade between two prices. You buy with patience and you sell with patience. The moment you start instant buying or instant selling out of frustration, you hand the edge to the players who kept their discipline. That is why the real skill is not finding the item name. It is finding a spread that survives fees, competition and the time it takes to get filled.

Liquidity changes what a good flip looks like. A thin item with a giant margin can still be worse than a resource with a tiny spread if the resource cycles quickly enough. Coins per hour, fill certainty and repeatability beat screenshot-worthy gaps almost every time.

## Decide whether you want active or passive bazaar play

| Style | What it looks like | Best fit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Active | Watching the spread, tweaking orders, reacting to short-term demand | Players who want to trade hands-on in real time |
| Passive | Liquid items, calm order placement, fills accumulate in the background | Players doing other content while orders fill |

Both work, but they should not be judged by the same expectations. If you keep mixing the two mindsets, you end up sabotaging both. Passive orders feel too slow, active flips feel too noisy, and neither gets a fair chance to work. Pick the routine that matches how you actually play.
