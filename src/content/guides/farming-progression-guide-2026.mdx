## Garden access is the real beginning

The cleanest farming advice in current creator coverage is to treat the Garden as your first true milestone, not a side activity. Once the Garden is open, farming becomes a system with visitors, copper, plot management and dedicated progression rather than random island harvesting.

That matters for money because the Garden compounds. Visitors push progress forward, copper unlocks better tools and upgrades, and better plot structure turns the same playtime into better output.

## Do not overspend on the wrong upgrades

One of the best practical warnings in farming guides is that not every visible system deserves your coins early. The composter can become useful later, but early overinvestment there often does less for your income than cleaner plots, better crop tools or stronger visitor handling. A good progression guide protects you from expensive premature upgrades.

<Callout title="Efficient early path:">
Unlock the Garden, stabilize your farm layout, improve the tools that directly raise output, and only then start layering on more expensive convenience systems.
</Callout>

## Visitors and copper are part of the money method

Farming is easy to misread if you only think in harvested crop value. Visitors and copper are part of the progression economy, not optional side flavor. The sooner you respect that, the sooner farming starts looking like a full account builder instead of a single crop loop.

This page is the umbrella farming guide for the series. Later pages like pest farming or melon versus wheat only make sense if your base Garden progression is already stable. If you jump into niche farming discussions before the platform is ready, you are optimizing noise instead of income.
