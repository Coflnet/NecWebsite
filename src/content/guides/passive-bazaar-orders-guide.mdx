## Passive flipping starts with item selection

The core rule is simple: passive orders only work on items that still move while you are away. If the market is shallow, the order might fill slowly on the way in and then punish you again when you try to exit. That is why creator guides keep returning to volume. A worse-looking spread on a liquid item is often a better passive play than an eye-catching margin on a thin one, because the liquid item can actually cycle capital repeatedly without forcing manual rescue.

This is also why low-touch bazaar routines pair well with players who already have another income stream. Farming, slayers or a few manual AH flips can occupy your active playtime while your bazaar orders work in the background. The money is not instant, but it is clean. You are essentially renting out patience to the market and collecting the difference between the two sides of the book.

## Patience is part of the method, not a failure state

Players often sabotage passive orders by intervening too quickly. If the spread was chosen correctly and the item is liquid, a slow fill does not mean the order is broken. It often means you are competing in a normal market.

<Callout variant="warning" title="Don't nudge every order upward">
The passive approach breaks down when you keep nudging every order upward, turning a patient strategy into a high-maintenance one that gives away edge through constant impatience.
</Callout>
